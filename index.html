üåæ Carbohydrates and Glycemic Index

üìö What is Glycemic Index (GI)?

GI is a 0-100 scale showing how quickly carbohydrates from a given product raise blood glucose levels. Low GI products cause a slower, more stable sugar rise, which is beneficial for:

  • ‚Ä¢ Maintaining stable energy
  • ‚Ä¢ Appetite and satiety control
  • ‚Ä¢ Better blood sugar control
  • ‚Ä¢ Reducing sudden insulin spikes
üü¢ Low GI (<55):
  • Leafy vegetables: GI <15 (spinach, lettuce, arugula)
  • Broccoli, cauliflower: GI ~10
  • Berries (blueberries, raspberries): GI ~25
  • Apples: GI ~36
  • Oranges: GI ~45
  • Lentils: GI ~29
  • Chickpeas: GI ~33
  • Beans: GI ~24
  • Quinoa: GI ~53
  • Pears: GI ~33
  • Natural yogurt: GI ~14
üü° Medium GI (56-69):
  • Oatmeal: GI ~55
  • Brown rice: GI ~50-55
  • Sweet potatoes: GI ~63
  • Bananas (ripe): GI ~62
  • Whole grain bread: GI ~56
  • Whole grain pasta: GI ~58
  • Buckwheat: GI ~54
üî¥ High GI (>70) - Limit:
  • White rice: GI ~73
  • Potatoes (boiled): GI ~78
  • White bread: GI ~75
  • Watermelon: GI ~72
  • Corn flakes: GI ~81
  • Glucose: GI 100 (reference point)

‚ö° Tip: Combining carbohydrates with protein and fats slows absorption and lowers the effective GI of a meal. Fiber also slows digestion.
